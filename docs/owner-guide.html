Welcome

What this guide covers

This document walks you through everything you need to run the Durham Stick Makers website day to day. It covers how visitors experience the site, how to sign in to the admin area, and step-by-step instructions for every task you will regularly need to do.

The site was built so that any member of the committee can manage it with no technical knowledge. If you can fill in a form and drag a photograph, you can run the site.

One rule of thumb: nothing goes live to the public until you explicitly click Publish. You can create drafts, upload images, and experiment freely. Delete or unpublish anything at any time.

The public site

What visitors see

The public website has eight main areas, all linked from the top navigation bar. Every page pulls its content from the admin panel, so what you change behind the scenes appears on the site immediately.

Homepage

Hero banner, featured sticks, next workshop, and a warm invitation to get in touch.

The Craft

An illustrated guide to stick types, materials, the making process, and stick care.

Gallery

Filterable photographs with a full-screen lightbox. Click any image to view it large.

Shop

Your product listings. Each stick has its own detail page with photos, materials, maker credit, and a buy button.

Workshops

Regular session times and a calendar of bookable workshops with online booking forms.

Journal

Blog posts - technique notes, member spotlights, show reports, and charity news.

About Us

Your story, mission, member profiles (pulled automatically from the admin), and the Heritage Crafts Red List.

Contact

A message form, your workshop address, opening hours, email, and social links.

Getting started

Signing in to admin

The admin area lives at /admin on your website. Go to your site URL followed by /admin/login and sign in with the email address and password that were set up for you.

  1. Open your website in a browser and add /admin/login to the end of the URL.
  2. Enter your email and password, then click Sign in.
  3. You will land on the Dashboard - a quick overview showing published products, drafts, pending orders, and upcoming workshops.
Tip: Bookmark the admin login page so you can get back to it quickly. If you forget your password, your developer can reset it through the Supabase dashboard.

The most important task

Adding a stick to the shop

This is the job you will do most often. Each stick is a one-of-a-kind listing with its own photos, description, price, and maker credit.

  1. Go to Products in the sidebar, then click + New listing.
  2. Enter a title (e.g. "Ram horn shepherds crook on hazel").
  3. Choose the product type (One of a kind, Supply, Gift voucher, or Workshop) and pick a category from the dropdown.
  4. Set the price in pounds. It is stored in pence internally so pricing is always exact.
  5. Upload photos - drag images into the upload zone or click to select files. Upload at least three: a full-length shot, a close-up of the handle, and the collar or ferrule. The first image becomes the listing thumbnail. Drag to reorder.
  6. Write a short description. Plain text - a few sentences about the stick's character, materials, and finish.
  7. Fill in the materials - handle, shank, and collar. The fields offer autocomplete from known materials.
  8. Select a maker from the dropdown so the stick is credited on the site.
  9. Tick Feature on homepage if you want this stick to appear in the featured row on the front page.
  10. Click Save as draft to come back later, or Publish to put it live in the shop immediately.
When you publish, the site automatically creates the payment entry in Stripe so the stick can be bought online. If you edit the price later, a new payment entry is created. You do not need to touch Stripe directly.

Editing and archiving

Click any product in the list to edit it. You can change the status between Draft, Published, and Sold at any time. To remove a product from the shop permanently, click Archive at the bottom of the edit page. Archived products are hidden from the shop but kept in the database for your records.

Events

Scheduling a workshop

Beyond the regular Monday and Tuesday drop-in sessions (which are configured in Settings), you can schedule one-off workshops, taster days, and courses.

  1. Go to Workshops in the sidebar, then click + New workshop.
  2. Enter a title, date, start time, and end time.
  3. Set the capacity (how many places) and the price (enter 0 for a free session).
  4. Add a description of what attendees will learn or make.
  5. Optionally upload a featured image.
  6. Click Save workshop. It appears on the public Workshops page immediately.

Viewing bookings

When visitors book a place, their details appear at the bottom of the workshop edit page: name, email, phone number, and how many places they booked. The spots-remaining counter updates automatically.

Tip: If a workshop fills up, the status changes to "Fully booked" automatically. You can override this in the Status dropdown if a place opens up.

Content

Writing a blog post

The journal supports a modular block-based editor. Instead of one big text box, you build your post from blocks - each one can be a different type of content.

Block types

Paragraph

Regular body text. Use one block per paragraph or group of lines.

Heading

A large section heading to break up long posts.

Subheading

A smaller heading beneath a main heading.

Quote

A pulled quote with an optional attribution line.

Image

Upload a photo inline with an optional caption and alt text.

List

Bulleted or numbered list. Add items one at a time.

Divider

A subtle visual break between sections.

Call to action

A button card linking to another page - internal or external.

How to write a post

  1. Go to Blog in the sidebar, then click + New post.
  2. Enter a title and a short excerpt (one or two sentences shown on the listing page).
  3. Build your content by clicking + Add block and choosing a block type. Use the arrows to reorder blocks.
  4. Add a featured image (shown at the top of the post and on the listing card).
  5. Optionally set an author name and category (e.g. Technique, Workshop news, Member spotlight).
  6. Click Save as draft to come back later, or Publish to make it live.
Old posts still work. If you wrote a post as plain text before the block editor was added, it will still display correctly. Opening it in the editor will show each paragraph as a paragraph block, ready for you to convert into headings, images, or quotes.

People

Managing members

Members appear in two places: the "Our people" grid on the About page, and the "Made by" dropdown on product listings. Adding a member here automatically populates both.

  1. Go to Members in the sidebar, then click + Add member.
  2. Enter their name and a short bio (a sentence or two about what they make or how long they have been involved).
  3. Upload a portrait photo - a candid at the bench works best.
  4. Set the display order (lower numbers appear first on the About page).
  5. Leave Active member ticked. Unticking it hides them from the About page and the product dropdown without deleting their record.
  6. Click Save member.

Communication

Reading messages

When somebody fills in the contact form on the public site, their message appears in Messages in the admin sidebar.

Unread messages have a brass dot beside them. Click a message to expand it - it auto-marks as read. Click Reply by email to open your mail client with the sender's address and subject already filled in.

You can mark a message as unread again if you want to come back to it later.

Photography

Tips for good photographs

All images on the site - product photos, workshop images, blog posts, member portraits - are uploaded through the same drag-and-drop system. The site handles resizing automatically, but better source photos make a better-looking site.

Use natural light

A window or outdoor shade beats a flash every time. Avoid harsh overhead lighting.

Shoot three angles

For each stick: full length, handle close-up, and collar or ferrule detail.

Keep backgrounds clean

A plain surface or a workshop bench. Avoid cluttered backgrounds.

Phone cameras are fine

A modern phone camera is more than enough. Just wipe the lens first.
